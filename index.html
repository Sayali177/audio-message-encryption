Guide How EchoCrypt Works
01
Compose your message or audio
Type a text message or attach an audio file (.wav, .mp3, .ogg). All processing happens locally in your browser — nothing is sent to any server before encryption.
02
Key derivation via PBKDF2
Your passphrase is processed with PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA-256 (100 000 iterations) along with a randomly generated 16-byte salt to produce a 256-bit AES key. Even weak passphrases benefit from the computational cost of derivation.
03
AES-256-GCM encryption
The plaintext (or audio bytes) are encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a unique 12-byte IV per message. GCM mode provides authenticated encryption — any tampering of the cipher is automatically detected on decryption.
04
Packaging & transmission
The salt, IV, and cipher bytes are concatenated and Base64-encoded into a compact, copyable cipher blob prefixed with EC1:. Share it over any channel — email, chat, QR code.
05
Decryption on recipient device
The recipient pastes the cipher blob, enters the shared passphrase, and clicks Decrypt. EchoCrypt re-derives the key, verifies the GCM authentication tag, decrypts the payload, and reveals the original message — entirely offline.
